INTERMEDIATE

ntermediate courses are typically taken by individuals who have completed the basic or beginner-level courses and want to further develop their skills and knowledge in a specific area. These courses aim to bridge the gap between foundational and advanced levels, offering a more comprehensive exploration of the subject matter. The content covered in an intermediate course often includes more complex concepts, theories, and practical applications. It delves into the nuances and intricacies of the subject, providing learners with a deeper understanding of the topic. The curriculum may involve case studies, practical exercises, projects, and collaborative activities to enhance hands-on learning and application of knowledge.

GRADUATE

A graduate course, also known as a postgraduate course, is an advanced level of study that follows the completion of an undergraduate degree. It is typically offered at the master's or doctoral level and is designed to provide in-depth knowledge, specialized skills, and advanced training in a specific field or discipline. Graduate courses are more focused and specialized compared to undergraduate programs. They delve into advanced topics and often involve research, critical analysis, and independent study. The curriculum is structured to challenge students intellectually and encourage them to contribute to the existing knowledge in their field of study.

POST GRADUATE

A postgraduate course, also known as a postgraduate program or a higher education program, refers to an advanced level of study that individuals pursue after completing their undergraduate degree. Postgraduate courses are typically offered at the master's or doctoral level and provide students with the opportunity to specialize in a specific field, conduct advanced research, and further enhance their knowledge and skillsExamples of postgraduate courses include Master of Science (M.Sc.), Master of Business Administration (M.B.A.), Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.), Graduate Diploma, and Professional Certifications, among others.
